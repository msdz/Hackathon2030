Dr. Jinfeng Zhou
Dr. Jinfeng Zhou is the Vice-Chair and Secretary-General of China Biodiversity Conservation and Green Development Foundation (CBCGDF). He is also an Executive Committee member of the Club of Rome and the World Commission Environmental Law (WCEL) of IUCN as well as a member of the World Commission on Protected Areas (WCPA) of IUCN, a member of IUCN WCPA Task Force on Other Effective Area-Based Conservation Measures (OECMs), Chief researcher of green development at the Internet Finance Research Institute of Zhejiang University and Vice Chairman of the World Green Design Organization (WGDO). He served as the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) for the ninth, tenth and eleventh session for 15 years, a Standing committee member of the All China Federation of Industry and Commerce, and Vice-Chair of the National Association of Vocational Education China (NAVEC) for the ninth and tenth session. Dr Zhou works hard to advocate promoted synergies between biodiversity conservation and climate change. His legislative advisory opinions have been largely heard by the policy-makers.

About DEV2030 Hackathon

As recently proposed "Smart Earth," we hope to fully utilize various intelligence technologies to perceive, integrate, and analyze numerous types of environmental information to make timely, intelligent, and scientific responses to ecological governance and nature protection demands. Environmental protection has a long way to go. We would implement the "open-source technology + environmental protection scenarios" model to contribute to ecological civilization construction and sustainable human development.
The hackathon will officially be launched on July 30. With the theme "D2030", our competition focus on environmental protection-related topics on multi-dimension, deepening the concept of "environmental protection + technology" in the development, promoting the harmonious coexistence within human and nature, accumulating for the "Smart Earth." OpenSource for the Earth. Making developments creates infinite possibilities, improves the earth's environment, and promotes humankind's sustainable development. Through promoting open-source technology and inspiring open collaboration, we believe it is possible to encourage developers to participate in environmental protection and contribute to environmental protection careers with their strengths. That is the reason why we launch the competition as an open-source hackathon.

FAQ

  • Participants

    no limitation (environmentalists, designers, artists, all developers in big data, AI, IoT, or blockchain are welcomed to join)

  • How to register and group

    Login https://hackathon.kaiyuanshe.cn/. After registration and approval, you will be invited to enter the official group of the competition for communication and team formation.

  • Submission Requirements and methods

    project introduction/Demo/Github code, etc. Please refer to the Open Hackathon Platform for details.
    Submission the works to: Open Hackathon Platform.

  • Competition questions

    Each group should choose at least one or unlimited number of topics. Available topics are updating...
    Special reminder: Works related to Ethereum blockchain technology will get extra points. The Ethereum Foundation may provide additional individual rewards. Other recommended technologies are updating...
    Topic 1:
    Volunteer Management and Remote Collaboration in Public Charity Organizations
    Topic 2:
    Sustainable Tourism (Low Carbon Tourism)
    Topic 3:
    Sustainable Agriculture
    Topic 4:
    Biodiversity Protection
    Topic 5: Other topics related to public environmental protection

  • Individual competition without partners

    after the registration is approved, you will be invited to enter the official group of the competition for communication. We will help you form a team and find partners.

  • I can't write code and also want to participate in the competition

    DEV2030 also has an environmental protection design competition. You can participate in the design competition and submit original works related to public welfare and environmental protection.
